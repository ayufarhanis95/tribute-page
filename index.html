The Late Selena Quintanilla Perez

1971-1988: Early life and career beginnings

  • Born on 16 April 1971 in Lake Jackson, Texas
  • In 1980, her dad, Quintanilla Jr., opened his first restaurant where Selana and her siblings would often perform
  • He then became manager of the newly formed band Selena y Los Dinos and began promoting it
  • The band was often turned down by Texas music venues because of the members' age and because Selena was their lead singer

1989-1991: Selena and Ven Conmigo

  • Jose Behar of newly formed label EMI Latin Records wanted to sign Selena after watching her perfom at the 1989 Tejano Music Awards while Sony Music Latin offered Quintanilla Jr. twice Capitol's signing fee
  • Selena released her self-entited debut album on October 17,1989 and it performed better than other recordings from other contemporaneuous female Tejano singers
  • Selena released her second studio album, Ven Conmigo, in September 1990
  • A registed nurse and fan named Yolando Salvidar asked Quintanilla Jr. to start a fan club and soon became a close friend to Selena and the family and also became acting president of the fan club

1992-1993: Selena Live

  • Selena released her third studio album, Entre a Mi Mundo, in May 1992, which was critically acclaimed as her "breakthrough album"
  • Selena released Live! a year after Entre a Mi Mundo

1994-1995: Fashion Venture and Amor Prohibido

  • Aside from music, in 1994 Selena began desigining and manufacturing a line of clothing
  • Salvidar managed Selena's boutiques after the Quantanilla family were impressed with the way she managed the fan club
  • Selena released her fourth studio album, Amor Prohibido, in March 1994

Murder of Selena

  • In Decemeber 1994, the boutiques began to suffer after the number of staff had decreased which according to staff members, Salvidar often dismissed those she disliked
  • Quintanilla Jr. began receiving calls from fans who said that they had paid for the fan club's membership and had received nothing. He investigated and discovered that Salvidare had embezzled more than $30,000 from the fan club and the boutiques
  • Selena met with Salvidar and demanded for the financial papers which then Salvidar took her gun from her purse and pointed it to Selena
  • Selena was shot as she attempted to flee
  • Selena was pronounced dead from blood loss and cardiac arrest after being taken to the hospital
  • Dreaming of You, the crossover album Selena had been working on at the time of her death, was released in July 1995
